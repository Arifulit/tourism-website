Built 400 years ago by Zamindar Said Khan Ponni, it stands as a testament to the rich architectural heritage of Bangladesh. The Atiya Mosque is a sight to behold with its exquisite architectural features. It is 18 meters long and 12 meters wide, with a large dome and three smaller ones. The terracotta walls of the mosque are adorned with intricate floral designs, showcasing the craftsmanship of the artisans of that time. The east and north walls are particularly remarkable, displaying the artistic brilliance of the era.
